BLIS-kenya

Basic Laboratory Information System (BLIS) Kenya

Introduction

BLIS (Basic Laboratory Information System) Kenya, is a fork of C4G BLIS an open source Web-based system that can be installed in a local, district, or national laboratory. It is a tool that helps to standardize laboratory procedures and standards, and to generate useful reports to give a realistic picture of laboratory services and assist with staff and budget planning.

Main Features

Intergration with machines

We have intergrated BLIS-Kenya with the Celltac-F machine and are currently working on intergrating more machines. Lab technologists are now able to to send results from the machine to the doctor directly, eliminating transcription errors and the need to print analyzer results.

Intergation with HMIS

BLIS-Kenya integrates smoothly with existing Hospital management systems, allowing for quick and seamless introduction of the labarotory information system into the hospital.

Other features of BLIS include

  • One-time entry of each unique patient.
  • Standardization collected data.
  • Customization.
  • Detailed reports.
  • Daily logs to be reviewed for data verification.
  • Simple data backup.

As with any properly implemented electronic record system, BLIS may be found over time to improve data accuracy and reduce costs in laboratories. Benefits already seen in labs using BLIS

  • Reduced burden for technicians, as opposed to paper system.
  • Improved consistency and accuracy of data entry.
  • Ability to view patient history and track samples.
  • Ability to aggregate data and analyze data patterns and trends.
  • Printed patient records in place of handwritten records
  • Printed daily logs that make the reports look like the paper forms used in the laboratory
